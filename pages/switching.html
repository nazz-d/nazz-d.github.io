Gigabit Switching & PoE+ Deployment

Modernized the lab's switching fabric by upgrading from legacy FastEthernet to a full Gigabit backbone using Cisco SG300-20 (Core) and Netgear GS728TP (Access) switches, adding high-speed inter-VLAN routing and PoE+ capabilities.

Cisco SG300-20 Netgear GS728TP Gigabit Ethernet PoE+ (802.3at) VLAN Segmentation L3 Routing

What I Built

  • Gigabit Upgrade: Replaced legacy Cisco 3750V2 switches (100 Mbps) with a full Gigabit fabric, restoring 1 Gbps line-rate performance between OPNsense, Proxmox, and storage.
  • Core/Access Hierarchy: Configured a Cisco SG300-20 as the L3 Core switch and a Netgear GS728TP as the high-density PoE+ Access switch.
  • PoE+ Integration: Leveraged the Netgear's 802.3at support to power the Ubiquiti Access Point and PoE-ready lab devices via a single data cable.
  • 10-VLAN Production Fabric: Carries the full 10-VLAN segmentation design (VLANs 2–10, 20) — including VLAN 7 DMZ isolation for Cloudflare Tunnel — using 802.1Q tagging across the multi-vendor switching fabric.
  • Secure Management: Hardened both switches by disabling Telnet/HTTP, configuring SSH access, and enforcing named local administrator accounts.

Rationale for the Upgrade

  • Performance: The previous 100 Mbps ceiling was a bottleneck for Proxmox migrations and Synology NAS backups.
  • Modern Efficiency: Moved from power-hungry, noisy enterprise rack-mount gear to more efficient, fan-managed Gigabit hardware.
  • Physical Simplification: Integrated PoE+ directly into the access layer, eliminating the need for standalone PoE injectors.
  • Multi-Vendor Mastery: Demonstrated the ability to maintain a consistent VLAN architecture across different network OS environments (Cisco IOS-like and Netgear Smart Managed).
Core Layer

Cisco SG300-20 Gateway

The SG300 acts as the high-performance Core. It handles the primary trunks to the OPNsense firewall and the Proxmox compute cluster at full Gigabit speeds. It serves as the STP Root for the environment, ensuring the most stable and fastest path for internal management and DMZ traffic.

Access Layer

Netgear GS728TP PoE+

The Netgear GS728TP provides high-density access and Power over Ethernet. By trunking into the Cisco Core, it extends the DMZ and Management VLANs to the rest of the rack. It powers the wireless infrastructure and provides 24 ports of growth for new physical hosts.

Isolation

VLAN 7 DMZ Continuity

Despite the hardware change, the security architecture remains: VLAN 7 (DMZ) is tagged across both switches. This ensures that the Cloudflare Tunnel container remains logically isolated from all other VLANs at the hardware level — regardless of the physical port it occupies.
